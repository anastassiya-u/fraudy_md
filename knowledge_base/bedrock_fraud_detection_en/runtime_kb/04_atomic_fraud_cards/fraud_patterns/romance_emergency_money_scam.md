# Romance emergency money scam

## Pattern summary
An online romantic contact builds trust and then requests money for an emergency, travel, medical issue, customs problem, or investment.

## Core deception
The user is led to believe sending money proves trust or solves a crisis for someone emotionally close.

## Common channels
- dating_app
- messenger
- social_media

## Red flags
- Fast emotional intimacy
- Avoids video or in-person verification
- Crisis story requiring urgent money
- Requests gift cards, crypto, transfer, or receiving funds
- Secrecy or guilt pressure

## Scam examples
- I need emergency help for travel; send funds today.
- Our future depends on this investment deposit.

## Legitimate contrast
Online relationships are not scams by default; risk rises when money, secrecy, or investment advice appears.

## What NOT to classify as fraud automatically
- Relationship has been verified offline and no money is requested
- Conversation remains social without financial pressure

## Safe user actions
- Pause before paying
- Talk to a trusted person
- Do not send crypto/gift cards/transfers
- Verify identity independently

## Runtime model instruction
Use this card across conversation history, not just one message. Raise risk when emotional bonding turns into financial requests.
