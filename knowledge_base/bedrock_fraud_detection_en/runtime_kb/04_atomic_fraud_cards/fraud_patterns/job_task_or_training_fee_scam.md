# Job task or training fee scam

## Pattern summary
A fake employer offers easy remote work or high pay, then asks for a fee, deposit, or paid task before employment.

## Core deception
The user is led to believe payment is required to unlock training, equipment, registration, or salary.

## Common channels
- job_platform
- email
- messenger

## Red flags
- Upfront registration, training, equipment, or task deposit fee
- High salary with vague duties
- Fast hiring without normal interview process
- Company identity or domain cannot be verified

## Scam examples
- Remote assistant role approved; pay training deposit to start.
- Complete prepaid tasks to unlock salary.

## Legitimate contrast
Legitimate employers do not require candidates to pay to receive work, salary, or training access.

## What NOT to classify as fraud automatically
- Recruiter verifies through corporate email and no payment is requested
- Documents requested after formal offer through official HR process

## Safe user actions
- Do not pay to get a job
- Verify company domain and legal entity
- Keep communication on the job platform until verified
- Do not provide bank/card data before contract

## Runtime model instruction
Use this card when a job offer includes pay-to-work, deposits, or unrealistic compensation. Raise risk if money or sensitive data is requested before formal employment.
