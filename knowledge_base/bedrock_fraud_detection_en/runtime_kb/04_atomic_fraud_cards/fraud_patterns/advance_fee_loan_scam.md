# Advance-fee loan scam

## Pattern summary
A loan or credit offer requires an upfront insurance, processing, tax, or activation fee before funds are released.

## Core deception
The user is led to believe a prepayment is necessary to unlock guaranteed credit or funds.

## Common channels
- email
- sms
- messenger
- web_page

## Red flags
- Guaranteed approval despite poor credit or no checks
- Upfront processing/insurance/tax fee
- Payment requested by crypto, gift card, or transfer
- Pressure to pay before offer expires

## Scam examples
- Loan approved; pay activation fee before disbursement.
- Send insurance fee to release your credit.

## Legitimate contrast
Legitimate lenders disclose terms and usually deduct fees from proceeds or charge through regulated billing, not random upfront transfers.

## What NOT to classify as fraud automatically
- User applied through a regulated lender and verifies terms on official site
- No upfront transfer to a personal account is requested

## Safe user actions
- Do not pay upfront fees to unknown lenders
- Verify lender license and official domain
- Avoid sending documents through unsolicited links
- Report attempted fraud if payment was requested

## Runtime model instruction
Use this card when a promised loan, grant, or credit requires prepayment. Combine with identity-data risks if documents are requested.
