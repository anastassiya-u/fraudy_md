# Small-sum request strategy

## Pattern summary
Fraudsters may request a small initial payment to reduce suspicion, test compliance, or harvest card data.

## Core deception
The user is led to believe the low amount means the action is safe or administrative.

## Common channels
- sms
- email
- messenger
- web_page
- marketplace

## Red flags
- Small fee before refund, delivery, job, prize, loan, or withdrawal
- Card entry required for a tiny amount
- More fees appear after first payment
- Payment goes outside official channel

## Scam examples
- Pay a small fee to confirm delivery.
- A small tax is required before withdrawal.

## Legitimate contrast
Legitimate small charges can exist, but should be visible in the official service and not requested through unsolicited links.

## What NOT to classify as fraud automatically
- User independently sees fee in official account
- No card data or external payment link is requested

## Safe user actions
- Do not assume small amount is safe
- Verify fee in official app/site
- Avoid entering card data from unsolicited links
- Stop if repeated fees appear

## Runtime model instruction
Use this technique card to explain why low-value requests can still be high risk when linked to card collection or fee escalation.
